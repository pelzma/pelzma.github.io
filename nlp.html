Natural Language Processing

The impacts of premise length on NLI models

Python, the ANLI dataset, and Google Cloud

Natural language inference classifies relationships between premise-hypothesis pairs. NLI models require training examples and the SNLI is one of the most commonly used sets. Previous studies found that SNLI examples may be suboptimal due to brevity of some premises. We explored impacts of premise length on NLI model accuracy by using previously published and author-generated NLI examples. We found that the inclusion of long-premise training examples may make models more resilient and that short-premise examples may lead to a skewed labeling distribution.
